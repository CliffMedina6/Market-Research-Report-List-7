<p><p>The brown sugar market is primarily categorized into light brown sugar and dark brown sugar. Light brown sugar contains a smaller amount of molasses, resulting in a mild flavor and lighter color, making it popular for baking and cooking. In contrast, dark brown sugar has a higher molasses content, giving it a richer flavor and darker hue, which is often preferred for robust recipes like gingerbread and barbecue sauces. Both types cater to diverse culinary needs and preferences in the food industry.</p></p>
