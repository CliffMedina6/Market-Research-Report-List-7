<p><p>The ground speed sensor market includes various types designed for measuring vehicle speed relative to the ground. Wire speed sensors utilize wire loops embedded in the road, detecting changes in electromagnetic fields as vehicles pass over. Traditional speed sensors typically rely on rotational movement of wheels to calculate speed. Radar sensors use radio waves to determine speed and distance, providing accurate measurements regardless of environmental conditions. Each type offers distinct advantages, catering to diverse applications in automotive and transportation sectors.</p></p>
