<p><p>The medical hand orthosis (HO) market encompasses two primary types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to allow for movement and facilitate functional tasks while providing support, often used in rehabilitation settings. Conversely, static orthotics are meant to immobilize and stabilize the hand, aiding in the healing process by restricting movement. Both types serve distinct purposes in treating hand injuries or conditions, enhancing patient recovery and improving overall functionality.</p></p>
