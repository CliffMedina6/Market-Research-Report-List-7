<p><p>The vehicle wheel spindle market is categorized into two primary types: driven wheel spindles and non-driven wheel spindles. Driven wheel spindles are integral to vehicles where torque is transmitted to the wheels, typically in front-wheel or all-wheel-drive systems, enhancing power and traction. In contrast, non-driven wheel spindles do not transmit power and are primarily found in rear-wheel-drive vehicles, providing stability and support without contributing to propulsion. Both types are essential for vehicle performance and safety.</p></p>
