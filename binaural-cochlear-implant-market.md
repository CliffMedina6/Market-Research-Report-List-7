<p><p>The binaural cochlear implant market is categorized into body-worn and ear-worn cochlear implants. Body-worn cochlear implants involve external components attached to a belt or clothing, providing flexibility and power for users with profound hearing loss. Conversely, ear-worn cochlear implants resemble traditional hearing aids, integrating components into an ear-level device for a more discreet appearance and enhanced comfort. Both types aim to improve auditory experiences for individuals, ultimately catering to varying preferences and needs in hearing rehabilitation.</p></p>
