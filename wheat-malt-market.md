<p><p>Wheat malt is categorized into two primary types: white malt and red malt. White malt is produced using lighter barley varieties, resulting in a milder flavor profile and pale color, making it ideal for lighter beers and baked goods. In contrast, red malt is derived from darker grains, offering a robust, slightly sweet flavor with deeper color, enhancing darker beers and specialty baked goods. Both types play crucial roles in various culinary and brewing applications, impacting flavor, color, and texture.</p></p>
