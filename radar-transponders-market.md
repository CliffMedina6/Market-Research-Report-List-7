<p><p>Radar transponders operate within various frequency bands, notably C Band and X Band, each serving distinct applications. C Band operates in the 4 to 8 GHz range, commonly used for long-range surveillance and weather monitoring due to its balance of resolution and penetration through atmospheric conditions. X Band, ranging from 8 to 12 GHz, is favored for applications requiring higher resolution, such as marine tracking and missile guidance. Both bands cater to military, aviation, and maritime sectors, enhancing detection capabilities.</p></p>
