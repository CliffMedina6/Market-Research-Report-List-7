<p><p>Potassium sulfate (K2SO4), identified by CAS 7778-80-5, is produced through various methods, influencing its market dynamics. The Mannheim process generates high-purity K2SO4 by reacting potassium chloride with sulfuric acid. Muriate of potash (MOP) is another potassium source, consisting primarily of potassium chloride. The Kieserite process uses magnesium sulfate to extract K2SO4, while brines from salt lakes provide an alternative source through evaporation methods. Each method impacts pricing, availability, and regional production capacities in the potassium sulfate market.</p></p>
