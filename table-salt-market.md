<p><p>The table salt market is primarily categorized into two types: halite and sea salt. Halite, commonly known as rock salt, is mined from underground deposits and often processed for purity. It features a consistent taste and texture, making it popular for culinary uses. Sea salt, derived through the evaporation of seawater, retains trace minerals, which can enhance flavor and color. This variety appeals to health-conscious consumers and gourmet chefs, driving demand for its natural and artisanal qualities in the market.</p></p>
