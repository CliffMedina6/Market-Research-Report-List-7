<p><p>The color chemicals market comprises several types of dyes, each tailored for specific applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, providing vibrant colors. Reactive dyes form covalent bonds with fibers, ensuring excellent wash and light fastness, commonly used on cotton. Sulfur dyes are economically viable for dark shades, particularly on cotton. Vat dyes offer exceptional color fastness, ideal for deep hues on cellulosic fibers. Acid dyes are suitable for protein fibers like wool and silk. Other market segments include specialty dyes for niche applications.</p></p>
