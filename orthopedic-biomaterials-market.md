<p><ul><li>Metal Orthopaedic Biomaterial</li><li>Non-Metal Orthopaedic Biomaterial</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The orthopedic biomaterials market is categorized into two main types: metal and non-metal orthopedic biomaterials. Metal orthopedic biomaterials, such as titanium and stainless steel, are favored for their strength, durability, and biocompatibility, making them ideal for surgical implants and prosthetics. Non-metal orthopedic biomaterials include polymers, ceramics, and composite materials, which offer benefits like flexibility, lighter weight, and enhanced biological integration. Both types are critical for improving patient outcomes in orthopedic procedures, addressing a range of musculoskeletal disorders.</p></p>
