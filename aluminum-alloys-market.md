<p><p>The aluminum alloys market is primarily segmented into wrought and cast alloys. Wrought alloys are processed through deformation methods like rolling and extrusion, making them suitable for applications requiring high strength and ductility, such as automotive and aerospace components. Cast alloys, on the other hand, are produced by pouring molten aluminum into molds, ideal for complex shapes and designs used in construction, machinery, and electronics. Each type serves distinct industries, influencing their demand and market dynamics.</p></p>
